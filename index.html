Executive Summary

Franktown SDA Church is preparing to build a wellness center focused on holistic health (body, mind, spirit). To maximise impact and financial sustainability, we propose the following six key components:

  • Full-service gym (strength + cardio + group fitness)
  • Hydrotherapy & sauna zone
  • Cooking-class kitchen + healthy lifestyle education
  • Micro-farm + “mini-agriculture/animal husbandry” classroom + weekly farmers’ market
  • Turf sports field (training, team sports, wellness programming)
  • On-site wellness professionals: personal trainer + massage therapist

Each component functions both as ministry (community wellness, outreach) and as a potential revenue stream (memberships, classes, market booths, rentals). Together these build a wellness campus where non-church members and the broader community can engage—helping offset building costs and offering natural outreach opportunities.

Below, each component is detailed with purpose, rough cost estimates, revenue potential, and mission alignment. This plan also adds a self-sustaining financial model (with free services for those in need), a rotating doctor/dentist clinic, and a Pathfinder camporee area.

1) Gym Component

Purpose & Mission Alignment

  • A full-service gym is a strong draw in the local community (Franktown/Elbert County).
  • Encourages health, wellness, discipline, community among members and non-members alike.
  • A “front door” for fitness visitors to encounter the church’s health ministry, faith messages, and classes.
  • Membership fees and personal training income help offset facility costs.

Facility / Equipment Estimate

  • Mid-sized gym (2,000–4,000 sq ft): $30,000–50,000 equipment.1
  • Larger setups (4,000+ sq ft): $100,000+ for full gear.2
  • Rule of thumb: ~$25/sq ft (e.g., 3,000 sq ft ≈ $75,000).3
  • Utilities for mid-sized 24/7 facility: $5,000–8,000/mo regionally.4
  • Franktown church context: plan $40,000–60,000 equipment + build-out.

Revenue Potential / Membership Model

  • $40/mo individual, $60/mo family; add-ons for PT and small-group classes.
  • 150 members × $40 = $6,000/mo ⇒ $72,000/yr.
  • 1 FT trainer: 20 sessions/week × $60 × 52 = $62,400/yr.
  • Combined modest scenario ≈ $100,000/yr gross from memberships + PT.

Suggested Metrics & Trackers

  • Membership growth/churn, ARPM, utilisation/peak hours.
  • PT sessions; maintenance cost/month; outreach conversion.

Implementation Suggestions

  • Consider leasing (~$2,000–3,000/mo for ~3,000 sq ft).5
  • Modular build; launch essentials first.
  • Member discounts; family/student/senior plans.
  • Use gym for wellness workshops (nutrition, classes).

2) Hydrotherapy / Sauna Zone

Purpose & Mission Alignment

  • Aligns with SDA health message; premium differentiator.
  • Package with memberships as recovery & wellness.

Setup & Cost Estimate

  • Commercial infrared or traditional sauna; ventilation/HVAC.
  • Hydrotherapy (warm pool, cold plunge, whirlpool): $50,000–150,000+ depending on scale.
  • Lean start: steam/sauna + cold plunge; plan utilities/filtration/cleaning.

Revenue & Mission Tie-in

  • Premium tier (+$15–20/mo), recovery packages.
  • Small-group events, retreats, sports recovery, education nights.

3) Cooking Classes & Healthy Lifestyle Education

Purpose & Mission Alignment

  • Nutrition central to church identity; practical discipleship.
  • Connects gym ↔ farm ↔ market for holistic wellness.

Setup & Cost Estimate

  • Demo/commercial kitchen: $30,000–80,000 depending on utilities.
  • Include maintenance, consumables, instructor staffing.

Revenue & Outreach

  • Paid classes ($25–40), workshops, kids’ camps, chef/nutritionist partnerships.
  • “Farm to table” via micro-farm; optional subscription recipe kits.

4) Micro-Farm + Weekly Farmers’ Market

Purpose & Mission Alignment

Creation care, education, and nutrition on site. Weekly market invites community and boosts outreach.

Terraponic / Hydroponic Systems

  • Hybrid hydroponic + aquaponic for high yield in limited space.
  • Urban hydroponic setups: ~$20–80/sq ft for advanced systems.6
  • Colorado climate favors greenhouse/indoor control for year-round production.7
  • Hydro crops: leafy greens, herbs, microgreens, strawberries; outdoor: cold-hardy veg, roots, berries; small livestock per zoning.

Costs, Yields & Profit (Illustrative)

  • 2,000 sq ft indoor + 0.5 acre outdoor: ≈ $100,000 startup (60k indoor, 40k outdoor).
  • Operating: utilities, seeds/nutrients, part-time labor, maintenance.
  • Example yield: 2,000 sq ft × 10 lb/sq ft/yr = 20,000 lb lettuce → at $3/lb ≈ $60,000 gross (plus herbs/microgreens).8
  • Outdoor produce/animal sales: +$20,000–30,000 (scale-dependent).
  • Farmers’ market: 20 vendors × $30 × 52 ≈ $31,000/yr stall fees; church farm stall ≈ $80,000/yr @ $2,000/wk × 40 weeks (aspirational).

5) Turf Sports Field

Purpose & Mission Alignment

Outdoor wellness, youth & family activities, leagues, and rentals—broadening outreach.

Setup & Cost Estimate

  • Small turf field (~100×50 ft): $200,000–300,000+ (base, drainage, turf, lighting, fencing).
  • Budget alternative: natural turf with markings.

Revenue & Outreach

  • Rentals: 10 hrs/wk × $100 = ~$52,000/yr; sports camps/clinics; church wellness events.
  • Premium gym tier with field access; free/low-cost community sports nights.

6) On-site Wellness Professionals (PT & Massage)

Purpose & Mission Alignment

Transforms a gym into holistic wellness, improves retention, and adds individualized care.

Setup & Costs

  • Massage suite + trainer assessment area: $20,000–40,000 build-out.

Revenue Potential

  • PT: 20 sessions/wk × $60 × 50 ≈ $60,000/yr; center retains ~40% → ~$24,000.
  • Massage: 25/wk × $80 × 50 ≈ $100,000/yr; center retains ~30% → ~$30,000.

Financial Self-Sustainability & Benevolence

Multiple revenue streams fund operations and benevolence, replacing recurring donations. Surplus supports a Community Health Fund to provide free gym memberships, produce boxes, healthcare visits, and youth scholarships.

Community Healthcare Extension (Doctor/Dentist)

  • Two exam/treatment suites (150–200 sq ft each), sink, privacy, ADA access.
  • Build-out: $40,000–60,000 for both rooms; equipment can be donated/used.
  • Services: preventive checkups, screenings, basic dental care, nutrition coaching, referrals.
  • Funding: Community Health Fund + modest co-pays; outreach to uninsured/low-income families.

Pathfinder Camporee & Youth Engagement (20 Acres)

  • Dedicated acres with tent sites, fire rings, water access, restrooms.
  • Integrate honors via farm, cooking, nature study, health/fitness, animal husbandry.
  • Area-wide camporees and Adventurer weekends; modest site-use fees for maintenance.

Marketing & Outreach Strategy

  • Brand as “Franktown Wellness & Community Center”.
  • Local advertising: social, flyers at market, school/league partnerships.
  • Free trial week / Open Day tours across gym, sauna, cooking demos, and farm.
  • Ambassador program; quarterly Health & Faith seminars.
  • Cross-promotions: market discounts; class trial passes; renter wellness offers.
  • Message holistic wellness (physical + spiritual + community).
